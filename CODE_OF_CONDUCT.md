# Code Of Conduct

KeySurgeon is a keyboard diagnostics tool. Discussions should stay useful for
people reporting hardware faults, privacy concerns, Windows terminal issues,
and repair guidance.

## Expected Behavior

- Be direct, specific, and respectful.
- Keep reports evidence-based: commands, versions, redacted output, and clear
  reproduction steps are better than broad claims.
- Protect privacy. Do not post typed private text, credentials, tokens, full key
  logs, or screenshots that expose unrelated personal data.
- Respect different skill levels. Hardware diagnosis, soldering, Windows
  terminals, and Python packaging all have different failure modes.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or repeated hostile comments.
- Pressuring people to share private typed text, credentials, or unsafe logs.
- Posting knowingly false diagnostic claims or fake proof output.
- Derailing issue threads away from the specific bug, board report, or support
  request.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments that expose private data or make
the project unsafe to use. Repeated or severe violations may lead to blocked
participation in issues, pull requests, or discussions.

For sensitive reports, follow `SECURITY.md` and provide only the minimum safe
reproduction details.
